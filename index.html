Let

Is the death of var. Never use it again. Rather than declare variables with var, simply 'let' them be. Fixes the previous issue of hoisting. Also, no reassignment here

Const

Con-st-junction-junction, what's your function? Const is a variable declaration that can be used in lieu of let. Const can be global or local in its scope and is a read-only reference. Const value cannot change through re-assignment and cannot be 're-declared' - it also doesn't like to share (name cannot be used elsewhere).
